# Zenza Technology — Brand Strategy

This document is the starting point for anyone working on Zenza's brand —
designer, marketer, or future hire. It explains who we are, how the company
and its products relate to each other, and how we want to sound and look
across every surface: website, product, pitch materials, and social media.

---

## 1. Company architecture

**Zenza Inc.** is the holding company — established 2022, the top of the
structure. It is deliberately separate from any single subsidiary and from
any subsidiary's own investors: Zenza Inc.'s role is to originate ideas and
launch them as independent companies, not to operate them day to day.

**Zenza Technology (ZenzaTech)** is one company under Zenza Inc. — the
technology subsidiary, building software products for African markets. It
is treated as a separate company, not a department: it has its own brand
presence, its own site, its own product roadmap.

**Zenza FID** (Fraud Intelligence Detection — or adjust to your preferred
expansion) is Zenza Technology's first product: a fraud-intelligence
platform for African banks and financial institutions.

Other companies under Zenza Inc., in early development as of this writing:
**Zenza Media**, **Zenza Advertising**, and **Zenza Branding & Design**
(currently developing apparel and brand content).

```
Zenza Inc.                          (holding company — zenzainc.com)
 ├── Zenza Technology                (tech subsidiary — zenzatech.com)
 │     └── Zenza FID                  (product — welcome/investors/careers pages)
 │           └── app.zenzatech.com     (the actual product console)
 ├── Zenza Media                      (in development)
 ├── Zenza Advertising                (in development)
 └── Zenza Branding & Design          (in development)
```

**Recommended naming pattern for future products under Zenza Technology:**
`Zenza [Short Product Identifier]` — e.g. `Zenza FID` (fraud), a future
`Zenza Pay` (payments) or `Zenza KYC` (identity), etc. This is a **branded
house** model at the product level: every Zenza Technology product visibly
belongs to Zenza Technology, and every product benefits from trust built by
the others.

**Two visual identities, one family.** Zenza Inc. and Zenza Technology
intentionally use *different* palettes — Zenza Inc. in black/white/cream
(editorial, holding-company gravitas), Zenza Technology in navy/teal
(technical, product-forward) — while sharing the same logo *template*
(circular dashed mark, wordmark with underline, mirrored reflection
beneath, a subline anchoring it to its parent). This is what makes them
read as a family without looking identical — see Section 3.

**How this shows up today:**
- Zenza Technology's site footer reads "A Zenza Inc. company."
- Zenza FID's pages read "Zenza FID is a Zenza Technology product."
- Every site links to the one above it in the hierarchy, and the ones below
  it — nobody is a dead end.

## 2. Mission, vision, positioning

**Mission:** Give African financial institutions a shared memory for fraud
— so what one institution learns protects every institution connected to
it, without any customer's private data ever changing hands.

**Vision:** To become the trusted intelligence layer behind every major
financial institution operating in Africa — the place fraud knowledge
lives, learns, and protects, regardless of which bank first learned it.

**Positioning statement:**
*For African banks and financial institutions, Zenza FID is the governed
fraud-intelligence layer that sits alongside their existing fraud tools —
unlike transaction-monitoring engines, which score individual transactions
but forget everything else, Zenza FID remembers, connects, and shares
safely.*

**Brand pillars** (the three things every piece of communication should
reinforce, in order of priority):
1. **Trustworthy** — we handle sensitive financial-crime data; nothing about
   the brand should feel casual, flashy, or overpromising.
2. **Clear** — fraud and compliance topics are already complicated; our
   communication should never add to that. Plain language over jargon,
   always.
3. **Quietly confident** — we're early-stage and honest about it, but the
   product thinking is serious and well-considered. The brand should feel
   substantial without pretending to be bigger than it is.

## 3. Audiences (and how tone shifts for each)

| Audience | What they need to feel | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| **General visitors / bank staff** (welcome.html) | "I understand what this does and why it matters" | Plain, warm, human — no jargon |
| **Investors** (investors.html) | "These people understand the market and have a real plan" | Precise, data-forward, technical where it matters |
| **Candidates / talent** (careers.html) | "This is a real opportunity, and they're being straight with me" | Honest, direct, low-hype |
| **Banks evaluating a pilot** (future sales materials) | "This is credible enough to bring to my compliance team" | Formal, evidence-based, calm |

The visual identity stays constant across all four — only the density of
detail and the vocabulary shift. This is already reflected in how the
website's four pages are written; keep that pattern for any new materials
(pitch decks, one-pagers, social posts).

## 4. Voice & tone — quick reference

**Do:**
- Use short, direct sentences.
- Name what the product does in terms a non-technical reader understands,
  then add technical precision for the audience that needs it.
- Be upfront about being early-stage where relevant (careers, funding) — it
  reads as trustworthy, not weak.
- Use concrete numbers over vague claims ("30%+ reduction in case
  resolution time" beats "significantly faster").

**Don't:**
- Don't use fear-based fraud marketing clichés ("criminals are everywhere,"
  "your bank isn't safe").
- Don't oversell with superlatives ("revolutionary," "world-class") — let
  the specificity of the product do the persuading.
- Don't bury the plain-language explanation under acronyms on any
  general-audience page.

---

## 5. Brand name pronunciation & usage

- "Zenza" — write it as a proper noun, capital Z, never stylized in ALL CAPS
  in body copy (the logo/wordmark may use caps as a design choice; running
  text should not).
- Always "Zenza Technology" for the parent company (not "Zenza Tech" in
  formal materials — that's fine informally, e.g. social media handles).
- Product name is "Zenza FID" — two words, both capitalized, no punctuation
  between them.

   same restraint, same "quietly confident" tone — using the templates
